TTRPG Class Insporation Generator

OVERVIEW

The goal for this website was to examine the possibilities of what we could do with AI in a website format. This comes form an exploritive curiousity and the limited forms of usage people tend to have with AI. I have a affinity for table top roleplaying games, and have found from time to time unsure of what I may want to play next or for a short series. So I had the idea of a page that takes in several Parameters, inside of a strict prompt, to give some suggestions on what the user could potentially want to play even if the user did not realize it. The end result was pretty interesting, and honestly felt pretty consistent and fair. However the main prompt needed to achieve this is quite long and exhaustive and ended up being the key linch pin in achieving this. If you would like to see the prompt open the dropdown just below.

Fill in AI prompt for website

"Treat all of the following inputs literally and exactly as written. Do not substitute with similar or generic options. All responses must use classes, archetypes, magic types, and mechanics ONLY from the system specified below. Do NOT reference Dungeons & Dragons, Pathfinder, or any other RPG system. I need to create a character for the fantasy tabletop game [System], but I am struggling to decide on what class to play. I prefer a [Position] position in combat. I am interested in combat styles like [Style]. Regarding magic types I have a preference for [Magic]. Please consider archetypes such as [Archetype]. Finally, also consider this as well in your decision: [UserData]. Which official classes in [System] best match my choices? Only suggest existing classes and do not invent new ones. FOR EACH suggested class, also generate 3–5 fitting CHARACTER NAMES. Names must match the tone, magic source, and archetypes described. Names should be setting-appropriate fantasy names (not modern, not joke names). Do not reuse names across classes. " + If none of the listed classes match well, you may suggest a CONCEPT BUILD. Any concept build must be clearly labeled as such and must not be presented as an official class. (editionNote? ` If classes are for Dungeons and Dragons 5e edition please specify ${editionNote})." Output Rules: - List each class as a clear header - Under each class, include a "Suggested Character Names:" section - Provide 3–5 names per class - Keep names concise and readable ` + `\n\nOutput format preference: ${format}.`

Input sections

What TTRPG system are you using

    Here I list a few standard and common options with some more niche but good systems that people may play

  • Dungeons & Dragons 5E
  • Vagabond, Pulp fiction roleplaying game
  • Pathfinder
  • Old School Essentials
Where on a battlefield does the user want to be?

    This falls into three main areas, with a fourth that chalks up to wherever works/multiple.

  • Vagabond, Pulp fiction roleplaying game
  • Backliner, ranged consists of things like an archer/gunner/spellcaster
  • Old School Essentials
  • Finally a midliner, which consists of things like rogue. These types can work in close or medium range sometimes preferring to move in and out of close range.
What role does the user like/want to fill in a party?

    This feels pretty straight forward but falls into physical combat, spellcasting, a mix, less combat and more supporting your allies, or anything. I left this for multiple select for those that were considering there options but not every option

  • Any, you are game for whatever
  • Martial, this can be anything from a knight to an archer to a gunslinger
  • Spellcaster, this is your Wizards, witch's, and Socerer types.
  • SpellSword, consists of Paladins, magic knights, a mix of physical and magic
  • Support, supports can be magical or non-magical. Bards, Clerics, druids to a degree even
Does the user have preference on where the magic is coming from?

    This section is really only for those that are curious about magic which is why the first option is any or none. As well as the fact that in some systems it can be flavor or a more specific class. For example in D&D5E Celestial could be a Paladin, Cleric, or even a warlock

  • Any or None, you are game for whatever
  • Eldritch, this refers to the strange and unexplained [a common depiction is Cthulu]
  • Celestial, Angel/Aether/Angelic like beings or often associated to light or good
  • Nature, as it sounds magic coming from nature or the natural properties of the world
  • Bestowed by a god, is fairly self explanitory however a key distinction is not if it is good or bad aligned god
  • Bestowed by a powerful being, this could be receiving power from any powerful person/being/godlike/demigodlike creature. Even mythical creature or monster
  • Ki or Chi, Often treated as the same thing. More the power within the body or soul is less magic and more a spiritual power
  • Arcane, the stereotypical depiction of magic from wizards or even in some cases sorcerers
Are their any archetypes or themes the user desire or has in mind?

    Some systemes utilize archetypes or themes in their character creation or when a player is creating a character they will use one as a outline for how they build their character. So this is the perfect spot for those, the AI then uses those in conjunction with everything else to try and make its suggestions.

  • Each entry should be broken up by a comma so it knows they are seperate points
Any extra or tertiary information the user wants considered?
  • This is for anything that doesn't perfectly fit into the other areas, or thought/guidelines the user has or wants considered.
  • For example maybe something the user knows they do not want to play or want suggested.
  • Perhaps even end goal and/or story arcs they have considered.
Finally how much detail or information is desired?

    This is to help manage the text response that is give to the user. Some may want thorough explanations with suggestions or direction on progressive devlopment, or just nothing else then a simple list.

  • First options is the full detailed response from AI. Usually consists of a list of suggestions, why those suggestions, and what it considers the most accurate suggestion possibly more a build then single class.
  • Condensed, cuts a lot of that fat, getting down to the suggested list, and what would be most accurate possibilities.
  • Nothing but classes is pretty to the point, it will just give you a simple condensed list of classes and virtually nothing else.
  • Expanded takes what full detailed has and then further extrapolates on the ideas. sometimes giving ideas for story archs for the character or even more builds that could additionally work. I have even in some cases suggest types of gods or power beings as outlines to develop a character further.
Response Box

    After pressing submit and some time has passed for the openAI to receive the information and process the information will be sent back to you in the box below submit.

  • The copy button when pressed puts all text into your clipboard so you can paste it elsewhere
  • The box itself starts off as a condensed box with a scroll bar. However when Expand is check the text box will open to full size. this gives the user options in how they wish to view the content.
